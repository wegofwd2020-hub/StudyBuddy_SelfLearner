## Risk read (preliminary, non-legal)

- **Primary risk = "Mentable", and it is moderated by field, not eliminated.** Both
  known "Mentable" entities sit in **mental-health / clinical care**; we sit in
  **educational-content software**. US likelihood-of-confusion turns on relatedness of
  goods/services, not just sound — so the different-field gap is a genuine mitigant.
  But the marks are **phonetically identical**, which is exactly the kind of call that
  needs an attorney's knockout opinion across classes 9 / 41 / 42 (+ EU, since the app
  is EU-based). **Net: Amber — unchanged from May, with more supporting detail.**
- **Exact-spelling green flag.** Nothing uses **"Mentible"** itself → the mark looks
  available and distinctive on the exact string; the conflict is purely the
  **near-miss "Mentable"**, not a head-on collision.
- **No new blocker found**, but **no clearance achieved either** — the register-level
  question (is there a *registered* "Mentable"/confusable mark in our classes?) is
  still open because the registers can't be searched here.
- **Secondary:** the crowded "Ment-" edtech space (Mentimeter et al.) is worth one
  line in the attorney brief but shows no direct hit.

## Recommendation

1. **Keep Mentible as the lead; do not lock assets/listings yet.** Status stays
   **Amber / conditional** (ADR-006).
2. **Commission the attorney knockout** — the gating action. Scope to give counsel:
   - Mentible vs **Mentable** likelihood-of-confusion across **classes 9 / 41 / 42**,
     US (**USPTO**) **and** EU (**EUIPO**, because the app is EU-based).
   - The two concrete "Mentable" entities above (NC clinical practice; RO Technovation
     app) + the "Ment-" edtech neighbours as context.
   - The different-field argument (educational software vs mental-health services) as
     the core question to pressure-test.
3. **Fallback ready if counsel says no-go:** **SelfSyllabus** (the May shortlist
   fallback; descriptive/weaker but no direct hit) — re-clear before adopting.
4. **Until cleared:** keep "Mentible" out of anything expensive-to-reverse — no store
   listing lock, no printed/embedded marks beyond the current app/web usage, per
   ADR-006's "conditional on clearance".
